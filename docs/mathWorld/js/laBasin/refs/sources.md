# LA Basin / Topanga Canyon — Sources Consulted

URLs consulted when building `laBasin.js` and `topangaCanyon.js`.
Retrieved 2026-04-21.

## Primary

- [Topanga, California — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topanga,_California)
  — Town setting, elevation (~732 ft / 223 m at post office),
  topography, notable buildings, culture.
- [Santa Monica Mountains — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Monica_Mountains)
  — Range extent (40 miles E-W from Hollywood Hills to Point Mugu),
  peak elevations (Sandstone Peak 3111 ft / 948 m), geology.
- [Topanga Canyon Boulevard (California State Route 27) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_State_Route_27)
  — Route from PCH to San Fernando Valley; winds through canyon.
- [Topanga State Park — California State Parks](https://www.parks.ca.gov/?page_id=629)
  — "Largest wildland within the boundaries of a major city." Oak
  and chaparral ecology.

## Topography & geology

- [Los Angeles Basin — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_Basin)
  — Context for the broader regional terrain.
- [Geology of Topanga Canyon — USGS](https://pubs.usgs.gov/of/1999/0017/)
  — Topanga Group sedimentary sequence; Miocene volcanics.
- [Topanga Creek — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topanga_Creek)
  — 11-mile creek, drains the canyon to the Pacific at Topanga
  Beach. Steelhead habitat.

## Landmarks

- [Topanga Country Store — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topanga,_California#Landmarks)
  — 1930s-era store at the heart of the town.
- [Will Geer's Theatricum Botanicum — official site](https://theatricum.com/)
  — Outdoor Shakespeare theater; tiered wooden amphitheater under the
  oaks. Founded 1973.

## Flora

- [California sycamore — Calscape](https://calscape.org/Platanus-racemosa-(California-Sycamore))
  — Creek-corridor tree; pale mottled bark, large leaves.
- [Coast live oak — Calscape](https://calscape.org/Quercus-agrifolia-(Coast-Live-Oak))
  — Dominant oak of the Santa Monica Mountains' lower slopes.
- [Chaparral — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chaparral)
  — Dry scrubland: chamise, ceanothus, manzanita, sage. Occupies
  upper slopes above ~250 m.

## Map imagery

## Venice Beach & Santa Monica Pier

- [Venice, Los Angeles — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venice,_Los_Angeles)
  — Town history, Ocean Front Walk, canals, Muscle Beach.
- [Venice Boardwalk — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venice_Boardwalk)
  — Technically "Ocean Front Walk", ~2.5 km concrete promenade.
- [Muscle Beach — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muscle_Beach)
  — Originated 1934 in Santa Monica; the Venice location is
  officially "Muscle Beach Venice."
- [Venice Skatepark — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venice_Skatepark)
  — 16,000 sq ft, opened 2009 on the former "Dogtown" skate site.
- [Venice Public Art Walls — Visit California](https://www.visitcalifornia.com/experience/venice-beach/)
  — Sanctioned legal graffiti walls.
- [Santa Monica Pier — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Monica_Pier)
  — 1909 Municipal Pier + 1916 Pleasure Pier; iconic entrance arch
  sign.
- [Pacific Park — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Park)
  — Oceanfront amusement park; Pacific Wheel, West Coaster, arcade.
- [Looff Hippodrome — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Looff_Hippodrome)
  — 1916 octagonal carousel building, National Historic Landmark.
- [U.S. Route 66 — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._Route_66#Beginning_and_end)
  — Santa Monica Pier is the symbolic western terminus.
