# UC Berkeley — Sources Consulted

URLs used when researching the campus for `ucbCampus.js`. Retrieved
2026-04-21.

## Primary

- [Campus of the University of California, Berkeley — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campus_of_the_University_of_California,_Berkeley)
  — Overall campus plan, creek description, Olmsted's design intent,
  4-quadrant zone breakdown, elevation range ("~50 ft to ~450 ft").
- [Sather Tower — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sather_Tower)
  — Tower GPS `37°52′19″N 122°15′28″W` (= 37.8719, -122.2578); 307 ft / 94 m
  height; its role as secondary axis in Howard's Beaux-Arts plan.
- [About South Hall — UC Berkeley School of Information](https://www.ischool.berkeley.edu/about/southhall)
  — 1873 build date (oldest on campus); "near the center of campus,
  near Doe Library and the Campanile."

## Campus maps (official)

- [UC Berkeley Campus Map PDF — berkeley.edu](https://www.berkeley.edu/map/)
  — Official interactive + downloadable map.
- [Berkeley Law campus map PDF](https://www.law.berkeley.edu/files/campusmap.pdf)
  — Static PDF version useful for quickly reading relative building positions.
  **Local copy:** [`ucb-campus-map.pdf`](ucb-campus-map.pdf) (1.2 MB,
  retrieved 2026-04-21).
- [Disability Access campus locator PDF](https://dac.berkeley.edu/sites/default/files/Campus_Locator_Map_24x182-print-final.pdf)
  — Labels every building with its acronym; good for cross-referencing.
