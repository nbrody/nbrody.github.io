January 2026

Lecture 1: Symmetric Spaces

Building on Elie Cartan's 1926 work, we define the foundations of symmetric space geometry.

Lie Algebras:

  • $\frakg$ is simple if $\dim \frakg > 1$ and it has no non-trivial ideals.
  • $\frakg$ is semisimple if it is a direct sum of simple ideals: $\frakg = \bigoplus \frakg_i$.

Examples:

  • $\mathfrak{sl}(n, \mathbb{R}) = \{M \in M_n(\mathbb{R}) \mid \text{tr}(M) = 0\}$
  • $\mathfrak{so}(p, q) = \{M \in M(p+q, \mathbb{R}) \mid M I_{p,q} + I_{p,q} M^t = 0\}$

The Killing Form

The Killing form $B(X, Y)$ is defined by the trace of the adjoint representation:

$B(X, Y) = \tr(\ad_X \circ \ad_Y)$

Cartan's Criteria:

  • $\frakg$ is semisimple $\iff B$ is non-degenerate.
  • $\frakg$ is compact $\iff B$ is negative definite.

Cartan Involution & Decomposition

A Cartan involution is an automorphism $\Theta: \frakg \to \frakg$ $(\Theta^2 = id)$ such that:

$B_\Theta(X, Y) = -B(X, \Theta Y) \quad \text{is positive definite}$

This yields the Cartan decomposition $\frakg = \frakk \oplus \fraks$ (where $\fraks$ is often called $\frakp$):

  • $\frakk$ is the $+1$ eigenspace (subalgebra of $K$).
  • $\fraks$ is the $-1$ eigenspace.

$B$ is negative definite on $\frakk$ and positive definite on $\fraks$.

Geometry of $X = G/K$

Let $G$ be a connected semisimple Lie group and $K$ a maximal compact subgroup.

The Metric:

$X = G/K$ admits a $G$-invariant Riemannian metric induced by $B|_{\fraks}$.

Geodesics & Exp:

  • The geodesic through $x_0 = eK$ in direction $X \in \fraks$ is: $\gamma(t) = (\exp tX) \cdot x_0$.
  • The exponential map $\exp_{x_0}: \fraks \to X$ is a diffeomorphism.

Curvature & Rank

Symmetric spaces $X = G/K$ of non-compact type satisfy:

\text{Sectional Curvature } K_\sigma \leq 0

Rank:

A maximal abelian subalgebra $\fraka \subset \fraks$ is a Cartan subspace.

The rank of $X$ is $\dim \fraka$.

Geometric Fact: The rank is the dimension of the largest flat $(\mathbb{R}^d)$ totally geodetically embedded in $X$.

Standard Example: $SL(n, \mathbb{R})$

For $G = SL(n, \mathbb{R})$ and $X = G/SO(n)$:

  • $\fraks = \{A \in M_n(\mathbb{R}) \mid A = A^t, \text{tr}(A) = 0\}$ (symmetric trace-zero).
  • $\fraka = \text{diagonal trace-zero matrices}$.
  • $\text{rank}(X) = n-1$.

$\Theta(X) = -X^t$ corresponds to $g \mapsto {}^t g^{-1}$ on the group.

Lecture 2: Subgroups of semisimple Lie groups

Let $G$ be a semisimple Lie group with trivial center, and $\frakg = \text{Lie}(G)$.

$\Theta \in \Aut(G) \text{ is a Cartan involution}$

The fixed set of $\Theta$ is a maximal compact subgroup $K \subset G$.

This determines a Cartan decomposition of the Lie algebra:

$\frakg = \frakk \oplus \fraks$

Decomposition & Roots

Take a maximal abelian subspace $\fraka \subset \fraks$; set $r = \dim \fraka$.

Let $M = Z_K(\fraka)$ be the centralizer in $K$ of $\fraka$, and $\frakm = \text{Lie}(M)$.

The restricted roots $\Sigma \subset \fraka^*$ give a decomposition:

$\frakg = (\frakm \oplus \fraka) \oplus \bigoplus_{\alpha \in \Sigma} \frakg_\alpha$

where $m_\alpha = \dim(\frakg_\alpha)$ are the multiplicities of the roots.

Weyl Chambers & Projections

A choice of Weyl chamber $\fraka_+ \subset \fraka$ determines:

  • Positive roots $\Sigma_+$
  • Simple roots $\Pi$

The Cartan projection $\kappa: G \to \fraka_+$ is defined via the $KAK$ decomposition:

$g = k_1 e^{\kappa(g)} k_2$

Finally, we set the half-sum of positive roots:

$\rho = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{\alpha \in \Sigma_+} m_\alpha \alpha$

Example 2: $G = SL_n(\mathbb{R})$

Let $\Theta(g) = {}^t g^{-1}$, so $K = SO(n, \mathbb{R})$.

$A = \{ H = \diag(e^{\lambda_1}, \dots, e^{\lambda_n}) \mid \sum \lambda_k = 0 \}$

$\fraka_+ = \{ H \in A \mid \lambda_1 \geq \dots \geq \lambda_n \}$

Roots and simple roots:

  • $\Sigma = \{ \alpha = e_i^* - e_j^* \mid i \neq j \}$
  • $\Sigma_+ = \{ e_i^* - e_j^* \mid i > j \}$
  • $\Pi = \{ e_{i+1}^* - e_i^* \mid 1 \leq i < n \}$

$A_2$ Root System ($n=3$)

For $SL_3(\mathbb{R})$:

  • Six roots forming a perfect hexagon.
  • Weyl chambers are the sectors between root lines.
  • Simple roots $\Pi = \{\alpha_1, \alpha_2\}$ define the fundamental chamber.
  • The Cartan projection $\kappa(g)$ always lies in $\fraka_+$.

Exterior Powers & Projection

The Cartan projection $\kappa(g)$ can be computed via exterior powers $\Lambda^k \mathbb{R}^n$:

$\kappa(g) = \left( \dots, \log \frac{\| \Lambda^k g \|}{\| \Lambda^{k-1} g \|}, \dots \right)$

where $\Lambda^k g \in SL(\Lambda^k \mathbb{R}^n)$.

Volume Estimates

Let $B \subset \subset G$ be a compact set (a "box"). As $\kappa(g) \to \infty$:

$\vol_G(BgB) \approx e^{2\rho(\kappa(g))}$

And for intersection volume:

$\vol_G(gBg^{-1} \cap B) \approx e^{-2\rho(\kappa(g))}$

Cartan Projection Stability

Let $B \subset \subset G$ be compact. There exists $r > 0$ such that:

$\kappa(BgB) \subset \kappa(g) + \fraka_r \quad \text{for all } g \in G$

where $\fraka_r = B(0,r) \cap \fraka$.

Proof Sketch: Stability

Using the exterior power formula for $\kappa$, we note that for any $b \in B$:

$\min_{b \in B} \| \Lambda^k b^{-1} \|^{-2} \leq \frac{\| \Lambda^k(b_1 g b_2) \|}{\| \Lambda^k g \|} \leq \max_{b \in B} \| \Lambda^k b \|^2$

For $g = e^{X_0}$ with $X_0 \in \fraka_+$, this implies $BgB \subset K e^{X_0 + \fraka_r} K$.

Taking logs, we get the uniform bound on $\kappa(b_1 g b_2) - \kappa(g)$.

Volume Proportionality

For $B \subset G$ with nonempty interior, there exist $c, C > 0$ such that:

$c e^{2\rho(\kappa(g))} \leq \vol_G(BgB) \leq C e^{2\rho(\kappa(g))}$

Proof Sketch: Using the Weyl integration formula for the $KAK$ decomposition:

$\vol_G(K e^{X_0 + \fraka_r} K) = \int_{X_0 + \fraka_r} \left( \prod_{\alpha \in \Sigma_+} \sinh(\alpha(X))^{m_\alpha} \right) dX$

As $X \to \infty$ in $\fraka_+$, the integrand is $\approx e^{\sum m_\alpha \alpha(X)} = e^{2\rho(X)}$.

2. Iwasawa Decompositions

Set $\frakn = \bigoplus_{\alpha \in \Sigma_+} \frakg_\alpha$. This is a nilpotent Lie algebra since $[\frakg_\alpha, \frakg_\beta] \subseteq \frakg_{\alpha+\beta}$.

Indeed, $\Theta(\frakg_\alpha) = \frakg_{-\alpha}$. We obtain the Iwasawa decomposition:

$\frakg = \frakk \oplus \fraka \oplus \frakn$

At the group level: $G = KAN$. We also define the minimal parabolic subalgebra:

$\frakp = \frakm \oplus \fraka \oplus \frakn$

Parabolic Subgroups

Let $P = N_G(\frakp) = MAN$ be the **minimal parabolic subgroup**, where $N = \exp(\frakn)$.

A subgroup $Q \subset G$ is **parabolic** if it contains a conjugate of $P$.

The space $G/P$ classifies minimal parabolic subgroups, corresponding to the **maximal boundary**.

$G/P = \{ \xi \mid \xi \text{ is a flag in } \fraka \}$

Example: $G = SL(n, \mathbb{R})$

Nilpotent part $N$:

Upper triangular matrices with 1s on the diagonal.

Minimal Parabolic $P$:

All upper triangular matrices (Borel subgroup).

Parabolic $Q$: Any block upper triangular subgroup containing $P$.

Theorem (Iwasawa)

Every $g \in G$ can be written in a **unique** way as:

$g = k e^X n \quad (k \in K, X \in \fraka, n \in N)$

The element $X = \eta(g) = \eta(g, P)$ is called the **Iwasawa cocycle**, satisfying the relation:

$\eta(g_1 g_2, P) = \eta(g_1, g_2 P) + \eta(g_2, P)$

where $\eta(g_1, g_2 P)$ denotes the cocycle relative to the shifted parabolic $g_2 P g_2^{-1}$.

2.3 Bruhat Decomposition

The Weyl group $W = M^*/M$ is generated by reflections:

$W = \langle s_\alpha \mid \alpha \in \Sigma \rangle$

For $G = SL_n(\mathbb{R})$, $W \cong S_n$ (permutations of $\{1, \dots, n\}$).

Define the opposite nilpotent part:

$\fraknm = \Theta(\frakn) = \bigoplus_{\alpha \in \Sigma_+} \frakg_{-\alpha}, \quad N^- = \exp(\fraknm)$

This gives a new decomposition $\frakg = \fraknm \oplus \frakm \oplus \fraka \oplus \frakn$.

Theorem: Bruhat Decomposition

  1. $G = \bigsqcup_{w \in W} N^{-} w P$
  2. $\Omega = N^{-} P$ is a Zariski open cell.
  3. For $w \neq 1$, $\text{codim}(N^{-} w P) \geq 1$.
  4. Every $g \in \Omega$ is uniquely $g = n_{-} m a n$.

Haar Measure Formula

The Haar measure $dg$ decomposes according to $N^- M A N$:

$\int_G \phi(g) dg = \int_{N^- \times M \times \fraka \times N} \phi(n_{-} m e^X n) \, dn_{-} \, dm \, e^{2\rho(X)} dX \, dn$

The factor $e^{2\rho(X)}$ rescales the right-invariant $MAN$ measure to a left-invariant one.

Proof: Intersection Volume

Recall $\vol_G(g B g^{-1} \cap B) \approx e^{-2\rho(\kappa(g))}$.

Sketch: Assume $g = e^X$ ($X \in \fraka_+$) and $B = N^{-}_\eps M_\eps A_\eps N_\eps$.

$g B g^{-1} \cap B \approx (g N^{-}_\eps g^{-1}) M_\eps A_\eps N_\eps$

Since $g$ contracts $N^-$, the volume is proportional to the Jacobian:

$\vol_G(g B g^{-1} \cap B) \approx \det \Ad_g|_{N^-} = e^{-2\rho(X)}$
