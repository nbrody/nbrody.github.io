The Martelli 5-Manifold

An interactive construction of the first known hyperbolic 5-manifold that fibers over the circle

📄 Read the paper (arXiv:2511.10530)

This interactive webpage explores the construction of a remarkable hyperbolic 5-manifold described by Bruno Martelli. The manifold \(M^5\) is the first known example of a hyperbolic 5-manifold that fibers over the circle \(S^1\):

\[F^4 \hookrightarrow M^5 \to S^1\]

Its construction brings together number theory (\(\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_5]\)), dynamical systems (Anosov maps), and geometric topology (Hantzsche-Wendt and Ratcliffe-Tschanz manifolds).

01

\(\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_5]\) — The Cyclotomic Ring

Roots of unity and the golden ratio

The ring \(\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_5]\) is the ring of integers in the cyclotomic field \(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_5)\), where \(\zeta_5 = e^{2\pi i/5}\) is a primitive fifth root of unity. Its minimal polynomial is the 5th cyclotomic polynomial:

\[\Phi_5(z) = z^4 + z^3 + z^2 + z + 1\]

The element \(\lambda = \zeta_5 + \zeta_5^{-1} = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}\) is the golden ratio \(\varphi\). Key properties:

  • Dedekind domain with class number 1 (UFD)
  • Unit group of rank 2
  • Full ring of integers of \(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_5)\)
  • \(\text{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_5)/\mathbb{Q}) \cong (\mathbb{Z}/5\mathbb{Z})^\times \cong \mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}\)

The Anosov Map

Hyperbolic dynamics on the torus

An Anosov diffeomorphism is a hyperbolic dynamical system on a compact manifold. One of the simplest examples is given by the matrix

\[A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\]

which acts on the 2-torus \(T^2 = \mathbb{R}^2/\mathbb{Z}^2\). This matrix has eigenvalues \(\varphi = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}\) and \(\psi = \frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{2}\). Since \(|\varphi| > 1\) and \(|\psi| < 1\), the map expands along one eigendirection and contracts along the other.

Expanding eigenspace \(v_+ = (\varphi, 1)\), eigenvalue \(\varphi \approx 1.618\)
Contracting eigenspace \(v_- = (\psi, 1)\), eigenvalue \(\psi \approx -0.618\)
Basis vector \(e_1 = (1,0)\)
Basis vector \(e_2 = (0,1)\)

The five fixed points \(P_1, \ldots, P_5\) of the map on \(T^2\) (from Theorem 7 of the paper) are tracked throughout. The animation shows:

  1. The linear map stretching the unit square into a parallelogram
  2. Cutting at \(x = 1\) and translating the overflow back (mod \(\mathbb{Z}^2\))
  3. The result: the Anosov diffeomorphism on \(T^2\)

The Hantzsche-Wendt Manifold

A flat 3-manifold from two cubes

The Hantzsche-Wendt manifold is a closed, flat 3-manifold with holonomy group \(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2\). It is constructed from two identical cubes forming a domain of size \(2 \times 1 \times 1\), with faces identified by four isometries — each of which moves the entire domain off of itself to tile \(\mathbb{R}^3\).

The four face-pairing isometries are:

  • Ends: \((x,y,z) \mapsto (x+2,\; y,\; z)\) — translation
  • Top/Bottom: \((x,y,z) \mapsto (2-x,\; y+1,\; 1-z)\) — 180° rotation about \(y\)-axis + shift up
  • Diagonal 1: \((x,y,z) \mapsto (x+1,\; 1-y,\; 1-z)\) — 180° rotation about \(x\)-axis + shift right (Front₁ ↔ Back₂)
  • Diagonal 2: \((x,y,z) \mapsto (x-1,\; 1-y,\; 1-z)\) — inverse of Diagonal 1 (Front₂ ↔ Back₁)

The Ratcliffe-Tschanz Manifold

Coxeter groups and hyperbolic reflections

The Ratcliffe-Tschanz manifold is constructed using the orthogonal group \(O(5,1;\mathbb{Z})\), which acts on hyperbolic 5-space \(\mathbb{H}^5\) by isometries. This group has a natural presentation as a Coxeter group.

Simplex Fundamental Domain

The fundamental domain for this reflection group on \(\mathbb{H}^5\) is a simplex bounded by 6 hyperplanes. It has 5 finite vertices on the hyperboloid \(\{x \in \mathbb{R}^6 : \langle x, x \rangle = -1\}\) and 1 ideal vertex at infinity:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \frac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \underset{\text{ideal}}{\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}} \]

Parabolic Subgroup

The 10 Ideal Vertices

Setting \(w = (1,1,1,1,1,2)\), the 10 ideal vertices (null vectors with \(\langle v,v\rangle = 0\)) are:

\(v_i = e_i + e_6\)
\(v_1 = (1,0,0,0,0,1)\)
\(v_2 = (0,1,0,0,0,1)\)
\(v_3 = (0,0,1,0,0,1)\)
\(v_4 = (0,0,0,1,0,1)\)
\(v_5 = (0,0,0,0,1,1)\)
\(w_i = w - e_i\)
\(w_1 = (0,1,1,1,1,2)\)
\(w_2 = (1,0,1,1,1,2)\)
\(w_3 = (1,1,0,1,1,2)\)
\(w_4 = (1,1,1,0,1,2)\)
\(w_5 = (1,1,1,1,0,2)\)

The reflection group generated by this Coxeter diagram is precisely the principal congruence subgroup of level 2, \(\Gamma(2) \leq O(5,1;\mathbb{Z})\), consisting of matrices \(A \equiv I \pmod{2}\).

The Figure-Eight Knot Connection

Branched covers and knot complements

The Hantzsche-Wendt manifold is the 3-fold cyclic branched cover of \(S^3\) branched over the figure-eight knot \(4_1\).

What does this mean?

  • Take 3 copies of \(S^3\), each cut along a Seifert surface bounded by the knot
  • Glue these copies together cyclically along the cut surfaces
  • The result is a manifold that "wraps around" the knot 3 times

The complement of the figure-eight knot in \(S^3\) carries a hyperbolic structure (decomposable into two ideal tetrahedra). The Hantzsche-Wendt manifold inherits geometric properties from this construction, bridging flat geometry with the hyperbolic geometry of the knot complement.

06

Synthesis: The Martelli 5-Manifold

Bringing it all together

The Martelli 5-manifold \(M^5\) is a specific finite cover of the Ratcliffe-Tschanz manifold. Its most striking property is the fibration:

\[F^4 \hookrightarrow M^5 \xrightarrow{\;\;\pi\;\;} S^1\]
🔷

Hyperbolic Geometry

\(M^5\) has constant negative curvature, constructed as a quotient of \(\mathbb{H}^5\) by a subgroup of \(O(5,1;\mathbb{Z})\).

🌀

Pseudo-Anosov Monodromy

The monodromy \(\phi: F^4 \to F^4\) is pseudo-Anosov — the 4-dimensional analogue of the 2D Anosov map on the torus shown in Section 02.

♾️

Hantzsche-Wendt Cusps

The manifold has cusps extending to infinity whose cross-sections are flat 4-manifolds related to the Hantzsche-Wendt manifold × \(S^1\).

Consequences

  1. There exists a compact smooth 4-manifold \(M\) such that no non-trivial class in \(H_2(M)\) is represented by immersed tori, yet infinitely many classes are represented by smoothly embedded genus-2 surfaces.
  2. There exists a compact locally CAT(0) space \(Y\) such that \(\pi_1(Y)\) is not hyperbolic and does not contain \(\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}\). This answers Gromov's Closing Flat Problem.
