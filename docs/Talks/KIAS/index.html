Surface Groups and Products of Trees

Nic Brody, UC Santa Cruz
January 23, 2026

Introduction

Suppose that \(G\) is a group.
Question 1: What are the subgroups of \(G\)?
Question 2: Which groups can \(G\) embed into?
Question 3: Which spaces can \(G\) act on?

The Goal

Our focus today is on the intersection of these questions for surface groups.

Classical Result

Every finite subgroup of the mapping class group \(\Mod(S)\) can be realized as a group of isometries of some hyperbolic structure on \(S\).

Background: \(\PSL_2(\R)\)

The group \(\PSL_2(\R)\) acts on the upper half plane \(\bbH = \{z \in \C : \Im(z) > 0\}\) via Möbius transformations:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \cdot z = \frac{az + b}{cz + d} \]

This action is transitive and preserves the hyperbolic metric \(ds^2 = \frac{dx^2 + dy^2}{y^2}\).

Iwasawa Decomposition

Every element \(g \in \SL_2(\R)\) can be uniquely factored as \(g = kan\):

\[ \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{pmatrix}}_{K \in \mathsf{SO}(2)} \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix} \lambda & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda^{-1} \end{pmatrix}}_{A \in \mathsf{A}} \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix} 1 & x \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}}_{N \in \mathsf{N}} \]

Geometrically, this represents a rotation, followed by a scaling, followed by a translation of \(\bbH\).

The \(p\)-adic Numbers

The field \(\Q_p\) is the completion of \(\Q\) with respect to the \(p\)-adic norm \(|x|_p\):

\[ x = p^v \frac{a}{b} \implies |x|_p = p^{-v} \]

In this non-archimedean world, a sequence converges if and only if the difference between terms goes to zero—leading to a totally disconnected topology.

The Bruhat-Tits Tree

The \(p\)-adic analogue of the hyperbolic plane is the Bruhat-Tits tree \(\mathbb{T}_p\).

  • Vertices are homothety classes of \(\Z_p\)-lattices in \(\Q_p^2\).
  • An edge exists between \([\Lambda]\) and \([\Lambda']\) if they have representatives with \(\Lambda' \subset \Lambda\) and \(\Lambda/\Lambda' \cong \Z/p\Z\).

This results in a regular tree of valence \(p+1\).

Action on \(\mathbb{T}_p\)

The group \(\PGL_2(\Q_p)\) acts by isometries on the tree \(\mathbb{T}_p\).

Just as in the real case, we have an Iwasawa decomposition:

\[ \PGL_2(\Q_p) = \PGL_2(\Z_p) \cdot \mathsf{A} \cdot \mathsf{N} \]

where \(\PGL_2(\Z_p)\) is the stabilizer of a vertex, acting as "rotations" of the tree.

Arithmetic Lattices

The Minkowski embedding allows us to view groups defined over \(\Q\) as discrete subgroups of Lie groups. For example:

\[ \PGL_2(\Q) \hookrightarrow \PGL_2(\R) \times \prod_{p} \PGL_2(\Q_p) \]

Let \(G\) be a semisimple algebraic group defined over \(\Q\). Then \(G(\Z)\) is a lattice in \(G(\R)\). More generally, \(G(\Z[1/S])\) is a lattice in \(\prod_{v \in S \cup \{\infty\}} G(\Q_v)\).

Approaches

Suppose \(\Gamma\) is a surface group. We will try to find a discrete and faithful representation of \(\Gamma\) into \(\Aut(\bbX)\) for some space \(\bbX\).

Long-Reid group

Suppose \(\Gamma\leq \PGL_2(\Q)\) is a surface group. By embedding \(\Q\) into the adeles \(\bbA\), we instantly have a proper action of \(\Gamma\) on

\(\bbH^2\times \prod'_{p\text{ prime}}\mathbb{T}_p\)

FLSS action

Suppose \(\Gamma\leq \PGL_2(\bbF_p(t))\) is a surface group. Since \(\Gamma\) is finitely generated, it is contained in \(\PGL_2(A)\) for some finitely generated subring \(A\) of \(\bbF_p(t)\). Such a ring is of the form \(A=\bbF_p[t,\frac{1}{f_1},...,\frac{1}{f_k}]\) for some irreducible polynomials \(f_i\in \bbF_p[t]\).

We can obtain a representation of \(\Gamma\) into \(\PGL_2(\bbF_p(t))\) by taking a representations of \(\Gamma\) into \(\PGL_2(\Z(t))\), and composing with the reduction map \(\Z(t)\to\bbF_p(t)\). If we can find a faithful such representation, we've succeeded!

Using the same surface group as before, we can consider \(\left\langle \begin{pmatrix} t & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \begin{pmatrix} t^2+1 & 2 \\ t & 1 \end{pmatrix} \right\rangle \leq \PGL_2(\Z(t))\)

Totally unitary representations

Suppose \(k\) is a number field, and \(\Gamma\) is a group. We say that a representation \(\rho\colon \Gamma\to \PGL_2(k)\) is totally unitary if for every archimedean place \(v\colon k\to \C\), \(\rho(\Gamma)\) is precompact.

Let \(\rho\colon \Gamma\to \PGL_2(k)\) be a faithful totally unitary representation. Then \(\rho\) induces a proper action of \(\Gamma\) on a locally compact product of trees.

Thus, if we can find a faithful and totally unitary surface group representation, we've succeeded!
For example, we can try to find surface subgroups of \(\mathsf{PU}_2(\Q(i))\). To keep things simple, let's seek matrices \(g=\begin{pmatrix} x & y \\ -y & x\end{pmatrix}\) and \(h=\begin{pmatrix} a+bi & c+di \\ -c+di & a-bi\end{pmatrix}\) where \([g,h]\) has trace zero.
This becomes the condition that \((x^2+y^2)(a^2+c^2)+(x^2-y^2)(b^2+d^2)=0\). We can find a particular integer solution \(x=4, y=5, a=3, b=1, c=3, d=9\), giving the representation \(g=\begin{pmatrix} 4 & 5 \\ -5 & 4\end{pmatrix}\) and \(h=\begin{pmatrix} 3+i & 3+9i \\ -3+9i & 3-i\end{pmatrix}\). Indeed, we note that \([g,h]=\begin{pmatrix} -\frac{27}{5\cdot 41}i & \frac{4}{5}-\frac{24}{41}i \\ -\frac{4}{5}-\frac{24}{41}i & \frac{27}{5\cdot 41}i\end{pmatrix}\).

Conjecture 1

Suppose \(k\) is a number field and \(\bbG\) is a simple \(k\)-algebraic group. We say that a subgroup \(\Gamma \leq \bbG(k)\) is:
\(\bullet\) algebraic if \(\Gamma\) is contained in \(\bbH(\Q)\lneq\mathsf{Res}_{k/\Q}(\bbG)(\Q)\) for some proper algebraic subgroup of \(\bbG(k)\).
\(\bullet\) geometric if there is some place \(v\) such that \(\Gamma\) is discrete in \(\bbG(k_v)\).
\(\bullet\) arithmetic if \(\Gamma\) is commensurable with \(\bbG(A)\) for some subring \(A \leq k\).

Conjecture:Every subgroup \(\Gamma\) is either algebraic, geometric, or arithmetic.
