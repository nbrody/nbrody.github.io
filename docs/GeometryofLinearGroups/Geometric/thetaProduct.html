Theta-Graph Product Without the Diagonal

Start with two theta graphs, each carrying one red, one green, and one blue edge. Their product has nine square cells ec × ed. Delete the three diagonal squares, and the remaining six cells become a closed genus 2 surface.

Construction
X = ⋃c ≠ d ec × ed

Equivalently: remove r × r, g × g, and b × b from Θ × Θ.

Vertices 4
Edges 12
Squares 6
Euler χ -2

Removing the diagonal leaves six squares. Since the cell counts are 4 vertices, 12 edges, and 6 squares, we get χ = 4 - 12 + 6 = -2, which is the Euler characteristic of a genus 2 surface.
