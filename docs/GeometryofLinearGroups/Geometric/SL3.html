How To Read The Picture

  • Colored vertices are lattice classes, with color determined by type modulo 3.
  • Radial distance from the center records graph distance from the standard vertex \([\mathbb{Z}_p^3]\).
  • The chamber mesh is drawn from the ball of radius \(r\) around the standard vertex, using lines and planes in \(L/pL\).
  • The bright path tracks the reduced word currently applied to the basepoint.
  • Click a vertex to inspect its canonical upper-triangular representative and its visible distance from the basepoint.

Model

A vertex in the building of \(\mathrm{PGL}_3(\mathbb{Q}_p)\) is a homothety class of rank-3 lattices in \(\mathbb{Q}_p^3\). This viewer keeps a canonical upper-triangular representative for each lattice class and uses your matrices to act on the base lattice.

Canonical Form

Each vertex is represented by a unique upper-triangular matrix \[ \begin{pmatrix} p^x & a & b \\ 0 & p^y & c \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \] where \(x, y \in \mathbb{Z}\), the entries \(a, b \in \mathbb{Z}[1/p]\,/\,p^x\mathbb{Z}\) are chosen in \([0, p^x)\), and \(c \in \mathbb{Z}[1/p]\,/\,p^y\mathbb{Z}\) is chosen in \([0, p^y)\). The bottom-right entry is fixed to \(1\) by the homothety normalization. Two lattice bases define the same vertex exactly when they yield the same canonical matrix.

Neighborhoods

Around a lattice \(L\), adjacent vertices come from nontrivial proper subspaces of \(L/pL\). For rank 3 those are lines and planes, and chambers come from flags \(\text{line} \subset \text{plane}\).

Animation

Generator buttons append or cancel letters in a reduced word. The highlighted tracker shows the corresponding orbit path for the basepoint. Sequence animation accepts tokens such as g1 and g2^-1.
