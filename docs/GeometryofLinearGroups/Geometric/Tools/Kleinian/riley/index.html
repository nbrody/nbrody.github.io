Riley Slice Explorer

Convention

Consider the group \(\Gamma_t=\langle S,T_t\rangle \leq \mathsf{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}[t])\), where \(S=\begin{pmatrix}0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix}\), and \(T=\begin{pmatrix}1 & t \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}\)

When \(z\) is a complex number, we obtain an evaluation map \(\mathbb{Z}[t]\to\mathbb{C}\), where we replace \(t\) with \(z\).

This map determines a map \(\mathsf{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}[t])\to\mathsf{SL}_2(\mathbb{C})\), and we let \(\Gamma_z\) denote the image of \(\Gamma_t\) under this evaluation map.

The Riley slice is the set of complex numbers \(z\) such that this map is discrete and faithful.
