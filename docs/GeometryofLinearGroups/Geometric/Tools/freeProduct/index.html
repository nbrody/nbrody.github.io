\(\mathbb{Z}^2 \ast \mathbb{Z}\) in \(\mathrm{SL}_3(\mathbb{Z}[\tfrac{1}{2}])\)

Generators \(a, b\) (diagonal, commuting) and \(t\) (non-diagonal) with \(\langle a, b, t \rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}^2 \ast \mathbb{Z}\).

Why \(\mathbb{Z}^2 \ast \mathbb{Z}\)?

\(a\) and \(b\) commute (both diagonal), so \(\langle a,b\rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}^2\). The matrix \(t\) has eigenvalues \(1, 2\pm\sqrt{3}\) with eigenvectors not aligned to any coordinate axis.

A ping-pong argument on \(\mathbb{RP}^2\): diagonal elements attract toward coordinate directions (red/green/blue dots), while \(t\) attracts toward its own eigenvectors (orange/purple dots). These regions are disjoint, so no reduced word in the free product can equal the identity.

How to verify

Enter any reduced word in \(\{a,b,t\}\) and check it is never the identity. For example, try commutators like a t a^-1 t^-1 or longer words. In a free product \(\mathbb{Z}^2 \ast \mathbb{Z}\), the only relation is \(ab = ba\).

Syntax: a b t a^-1 b^2 t^-1 (space-separated).
