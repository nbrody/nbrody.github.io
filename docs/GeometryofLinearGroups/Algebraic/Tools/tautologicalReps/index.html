Tautological Representations

Exploring the universal representations of groups over function fields and their specializations.

The Universal Perspective

A tautological representation is a map \(\rho: \Gamma \to \mathsf{SL}_n(R)\) where the matrix entries in \(\mathsf{SL}_n(R)\) are themselves the "coordinates" of the representation. Typically, \(R\) is a ring of functions (like \(\mathbb{Z}[x_1, \dots, x_k]\)) on some algebraic variety \(X\) related to the group \(\Gamma\).

By evaluating these coordinates at a point \(p \in X\), we obtain a specialization: a concrete representation into \(\mathsf{SL}_n(\mathbb{C})\) or another field. The most famous examples come from the character variety of 3-manifold groups.

The Torus Orbifold (\(\mathbb{Z}^2\))

For a torus, the fundamental group is \(\mathbb{Z}^2 = \langle a, b \mid [a, b] = 1 \rangle\). The tautological representation \(\rho: \mathbb{Z}^2 \to \mathsf{SL}_2(\mathbb{C})\) sends the generators to commuting matrices. In the parabolic case, we can write:

\[ \rho(a) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \rho(b) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & z \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]

As \(z\) varies in the upper half-plane \(\mathbb{H}\), we recover the different conformal structures of the torus.

The Figure Eight Knot complement

The figure-eight knot group has a presentation \(\Gamma = \langle s, t \mid s w = w t \rangle\) where \(w = s t^{-1} s^{-1} t\). The Riley representation into \(\mathsf{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}[z, z^{-1}])\) is given by:

\[ \rho(s) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \rho(t) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ z & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]

The Riley slice consists of \(z \in \mathbb{C}\) where this representation is discrete and faithful. The Figure Eight knot corresponds to the specialization at \(z = e^{i\pi/3} = \frac{1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2}\).
