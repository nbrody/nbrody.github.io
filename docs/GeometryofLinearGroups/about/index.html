About This Project

This collection of interactive tools explores the rich connections between algebra, geometry, and arithmetic in the study of linear groups. The project aims to make advanced mathematical concepts accessible through visualization and computation.

What are Linear Groups?

Linear groups are groups of invertible matrices. They appear throughout mathematics, connecting algebra, geometry, number theory, and topology in profound ways.

Three Perspectives

The project is organized around three complementary viewpoints: algebraic structure, geometric actions, and arithmetic properties of linear groups.

p-adic groups

Each tool provides hands-on exploration of mathematical objects, from visualizing hyperbolic geometry to computing group presentations and exploring quaternion algebras.

Research Context

These tools grew out of research in geometric group theory, particularly the study of lattices in Lie groups and their connections to number theory.

Open Problems & Conjectures

Explore open questions and conjectures in the field of linear groups and geometric group theory

Technologies Used

Built with Three.js for 3D visualization, MathQuill for mathematical typesetting, and custom JavaScript for mathematical computations.

Who Is This For?

Graduate students and researchers in geometric group theory, number theory, and related fields. Some tools may also interest advanced undergraduates.

About the Author

Created by Nic Brody, PhD student in Mathematics at UC Santa Cruz, studying arithmetic groups and their geometric properties.
