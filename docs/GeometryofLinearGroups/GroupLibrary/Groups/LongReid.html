The Long-Reid Group: \(\left\langle \frac{1}{3}\begin{pmatrix} 9 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \tfrac{1}{8}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 9 & 82 \end{pmatrix}\right\rangle\)

The Long-Reid group is a specific surface subgroup of \(PGL_2(\mathbb{Q})\). It has a natural action on a product of locally finite trees, and it is a longstanding question whether this is a proper action.

Consider the orbifold which is a torus with a single cone point of order 2. The fundamental group \(\Gamma\) of this orbifold may be presented as \(\langle a,b \mid (aba^{-1}b^{-1})^2 = 1\rangle \). To find a representation of $\Gamma$ into \(PGL_2(\mathbb{Q})\), we start with \( a = \begin{pmatrix} t & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix} \). In order for the relation to hold, we require that the trace of \(c=[a,b]=aba^{-1}b^{-1}\) is 0. With our choice of \(a\), this forces the entries of \(b\) to satisfy \( 2b_{11}b_{22}=(t+t^{-1})b_{12}b_{21} \). We can choose \( b = \begin{pmatrix} t^2+1 & 2 \\ t & 1\end{pmatrix} \), and then \(c = \begin{pmatrix} t^2+1 & 2t \\ 1 & 1\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ -t & t^2+1 \end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix} 1-t^2 & -2(t^2+1)(1-t) \\ 1-t & -(1-t^2) \end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix} 1+t & -2(t^2+1) \\ 1 & -(1+t) \end{pmatrix} \). We obtain a subgroup of \(PGL_2(\mathbb{Z}[t,\frac{1}{t},\frac{1}{t-1}])\) isomorphic to \(\Gamma\). By setting \(t=9\), we obtain the group studied by Long and Reid, which is contained in \(PSL_2(\mathbb{Z}[1/6])\). This group acts on each of the trees for \(PGL_2(\mathbb{Q}_2)\) and \(PGL_2(\mathbb{Q}_3)\), and the action is proper if and only if the intersection of \(\Gamma\) with \(PSL_2(\mathbb{Z})\) is finite.
