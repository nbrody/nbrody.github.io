\(\mathsf{Isom}_n(A)=\mathsf{SO}_n(A)\ltimes A^n\)

The affine group \(\mathsf{SO}_n(A)\ltimes A^n\) is a subgroup of \(SL_{n+1}(A)\) for any commutative ring \(A\). This can serve as an interesting example of a linear group which satisfies some property but not another, in various hierarchies of properties.

Let us first focus on the case where \(n=3\) and \(A=\mathbb{Z}[1/65]\). The group \(\mathsf{SO}_3(\mathbb{Z}[1/65])\) is a lattice in a product of trees, and it is maximal in the group \(\mathsf{Isom}_3(\mathbb{Z}[1/65])\).
