Geometry of Linear Groups

This site provides interactive tools for exploring the world of linear groups, including algebraic, geometric, and arithmetic aspects.

Choose a category to get started:

Let \( k \) be a number field, and \( \mathbb{G} \) a simple \( k \)-algebraic group. We say that a subgroup \( \Gamma \) of \( \mathbb{G}(k) \) is:


algebraic if it is contained in a proper \( \mathbb{Q} \)-algebraic subgroup of \( \mathrm{Res}_{k/\mathbb{Q}} \mathbb{G} \),
geometric if there is a completion \( k_v \) of \( k \) so that \( \Gamma \) is discrete in \( \mathbb{G}(k_v) \),
arithmetic if there is a subring \( A \subset k \) so that \( \Gamma \) is commensurable with \( \mathbb{G}(A) \).
We conjecture that every \(\Gamma\) is either algebraic, geometric, or arithmetic.
