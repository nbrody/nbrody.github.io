\( \text{SL}(3, \mathbb{Z}) \) Relation Search

About this Search

This tool searches for non-trivial relations in subgroups of \( \text{SL}(3, \mathbb{Z}) \). Specifically, we explore the group \( G = \langle A^k, B^m, T' \rangle \).

1. The Generators

The matrices \( A \) and \( B \) are defined as:

$$ A = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} $$

Note that \( B = A^2 - 2I \). These matrices arise from Dirichlet units of a cyclic cubic field.

The matrix \( T = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 & 6 \\ 2 & 3 & 4 \\ 2 & 3 & 5 \end{pmatrix} \) can be modified via elementary row operations.

2. The Search Algorithm

  • Phase 1 (Geometric Invariant Search): We search to find a "quasi-unipotent" element \( P \). We define \( P \) as quasi-unipotent if \( P^2 \) is unipotent (characteristic polynomial \( (x-1)^3 \)). If so, the kernel of \( (P^2 - I) \) defines an invariant plane. We compute its normal vector \( n \).
  • Phase 2 (Stabilizer Search): We then search for a new element \( Q \in G \) that stabilizes this plane (i.e., \( Q^T n \parallel n \)).
  • Finding Relations: If \( Q \) stabilizes the plane fixed by \( P^2 \), the conjugate \( P' = Q P^2 Q^{-1} \) also preserves that plane. Two such transformations often commute. We check for the relation: $$ [P^2, Q P^2 Q^{-1}] = I $$
